Who Is Kraig Gordon

Protect and Preserve the Lowcountry Quality of Life

I've called Beaufort County home for 33 years, living in both Hilton Head and Bluffton. But I'm not just a resident — I'm a proven servant. As the current Chairman of the Beaufort County Transportation Committee, I don't just know we have traffic problems — I'm in the trenches solving them.

My values are a product of my commitment, not a seasonal talking point. With 25 years of service in the U.S. Army and a career as a Civil Engineer, I've spent my life solving hard problems with precision, accountability, and care.

I hear your concerns, and I have clear plans to balance our growth while preserving the culture, nature, and quality of life we all love. I won't need a 'grace period' to learn the ropes — I will hit the ground running on Day One.

Kraig's Transportation Plan

Engineering Solutions for Real Problems

Kraig's Growth Plan

Stop Reacting. Start Managing.

Beaufort County is growing whether we like it or not. Kraig Gordon's plan ensures that growth strengthens — rather than strains — our community.

Curb Cut Management

Kraig will implement strict "Curb Cut" policies off County Roads to maintain traffic flow and safety. Too many poorly placed curb cuts are already choking our main roads — this stops.

Service Road Requirements

Kraig will require developers to provide service roads so new developments don't bottleneck our main thoroughfares. If you build it, you fund the access — not the taxpayer.

Modernizing Impact Fees

Developer Impact Fees haven't been updated in years. Kraig will ensure developers pay their fair share to support the infrastructure their projects require. Growth should pay for growth.

Kraig's Economic Vision

The Right Business for the Lowcountry

Economic growth is a double-edged sword. Kraig Gordon believes we must seek a balanced approach that preserves our unique Lowcountry way of life.

Selective Growth

We don't just need any business; we need the right businesses that our limited resources can sustain. Every economic development opportunity evaluated through the lens of long-term community benefit.

Environmental & School Impact

Every new business must be evaluated for its impact on our local schools and our fragile environment. The Lowcountry's marshes, waterways, and natural beauty are the foundation of our economy and quality of life.

Protecting the Lowcountry Way of Life

The character of our community — the pace, the culture, the natural beauty — is worth protecting. Economic decisions made today will define the Lowcountry for the next generation.
